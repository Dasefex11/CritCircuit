The Golden Age of Asmongold

Written by John Sullivan

I have been watching Asmongold since 2017, and I will be honest: now that so much of his content has moved into political commentary, it feels stale compared to what made his streams special in the first place.

It makes me wonder if we already lived through the golden age of Asmongold.

Recently, Asmongold and McConnell playing World of Warcraft again felt like a breath of fresh air. It brought back the energy of Classic WoW, when the stream felt unpredictable, funny, and full of characters that made the whole thing bigger than just one person playing a game.

When I think about the golden age of Asmongold, I think of 2019. Classic WoW launched, and Asmongold was pulling massive crowds. That run was genuinely a treat, and it is still sorely missed.

One of the funniest moments was launch day, when Asmongold got kicked from the game in front of a massive audience and ended up sleeping on stream while waiting to reconnect. Eventually, Esfand woke him up. It was ridiculous, but that was part of the magic. The stream felt alive even when nothing was technically happening.

We got content like ban appeals, ninja-loot drama, and raids that turned into even more loot drama. I remember sitting in my office, completely distracted, watching all of this unfold from a guy who lived with his mom and somehow seemed to be living the life we all wished we had.

And it was not just Asmongold carrying it alone. McConnell was a huge part of what made that era work. Their back-and-forth gave the streams a different kind of energy. Then you had people outside his inner circle who became part of the story too.

There was Jigsaw, who took over Olympus 4, one of Asmongold's Classic WoW guilds, and told everyone he was going to build a wall. After Asmongold interviewed him, Jigsaw showed off the wall of Stormwind. It was the kind of random player-driven content you could never script.

Then there was AdvertiseSP, one of the most memorable names from that era. He constantly harassed Asmongold in-game, ganked him, and turned into a running feud that lasted for weeks. To this day, he feels like one of those Classic WoW legends who appeared, caused chaos, and disappeared.

That era also gave us some of the best WoW community content. I still remember Asmongold watching Barny's Scarab Lord Adventures, along with videos from Happy Camper, which captured exactly why Classic WoW felt like a world full of stories instead of just another game launch.

That is what feels missing now. The golden age of Asmongold was not just about WoW. It was about the mix of the game, the community, McConnell, random players, stream moments, and stories that kept building on themselves.

Political commentary might get views, but Classic WoW gave us moments people still talk about years later.

And that is why, for me, the golden age of Asmongold will always be tied to World of Warcraft.
